# Vestigia — Guide ○ A  
◆ Recording the Work as It Happens

---

## Ⅰ — Why This Guide Exists

Most final‑year projects fail to document *how* the work happened.

Not because students do not work hard —  
but because the thinking, decisions, revisions, and failures are rarely captured **while they occur**.

Vestigia — Guide ○ A exists to help you:

◆ record your project as a living process  
◇ preserve the thinking behind your decisions  
◈ reduce stress at report‑writing time  
◉ turn daily work into professional evidence  

This guide is not about writing more.  
It is about **writing earlier, lighter, and honestly**.

---

## Ⅱ — Who This Guide Is For

Guide ○ A is written primarily for students in:

◆ Computer Science  
◆ Information Technology  
◆ Engineering (software, electrical, mechanical, systems)  

However, the practices in this guide also apply to:

◇ research‑led projects  
◇ applied business or industry projects  
◇ data, AI, and analytics work  
◇ education, design, or interdisciplinary work  

If your project involves **thinking, decisions, and iteration**, this guide applies to you.

---

## Ⅲ — What You Are Expected to Record

You are **not** recording everything.

You are recording **meaningful moments in the work**.

Use Vestigia to capture the following categories:

---

### Ⅲ.Ⅰ — Direction & Intention

Record moments where you decide *what you are trying to do*.

Examples:

✻ defining or refining your problem  
✻ clarifying requirements or goals  
✻ narrowing scope  
✻ deciding what not to do  

Write briefly:

◆ what you are aiming for  
◆ why this direction matters  
◇ what assumptions you are making  

---

### Ⅲ.Ⅱ — Decisions & Trade‑offs

Every project involves choosing *one path over others*.

Record:

✻ technical decisions  
✻ design choices  
✻ research directions  
✻ tool or methodology selection  

For each decision, capture:

⩪ what you chose  
⩫ alternatives you considered  
⩬ why you decided this way  
⩭ what risks or limitations you accept  

This applies whether you are choosing:

◆ a framework  
◆ an algorithm  
◆ a dataset  
◆ a design pattern  
◆ a research method  

---

### Ⅲ.Ⅲ — Work Performed

Record **what you actually did**, not what you planned.

Examples:

✻ implemented feature X  
✻ tested approach Y  
✻ ran experiment Z  
✻ analysed dataset  
✻ built prototype  

Avoid diaries.

Instead, write short summaries that answer:

⩰ what changed since last entry  
⩱ what moved forward  
⩲ what blocked progress  

---

### Ⅲ.Ⅳ — Problems, Failure & Rework

Failure is **required**, not embarrassing.

Record:

✻ things that did not work  
✻ bugs, dead‑ends, invalid assumptions  
✻ incorrect models or designs  
✻ rejected ideas  

For each failure, note:

⩳ what failed  
⩴ why you think it failed  
⩵ what you learned  
⩶ what you will try next  

This is often the **most valuable** part of your project.

---

### Ⅲ.Ⅴ — Reflection in Motion

Do not save reflection for the end.

Throughout the project, record:

✻ moments of insight  
✻ shifts in understanding  
✻ skills developing  
✻ changes in confidence  

Simple prompts are enough:

◇ “I expected X, but observed Y.”  
◇ “This changed how I think about…”  
◇ “If I restarted, I would…”  

These entries later become:
◆ reflection sections  
◆ presentation narratives  
◆ interview material  

---

## Ⅳ — How Often to Write

There is no fixed rule — but consistency matters.

Use these guidelines:

⚊ after meaningful work sessions  
⚋ after decisions are made  
⚎ after problems or breakthroughs  
⚏ at least once per week  

Short entries written regularly are **far better** than long entries written late.

---

## Ⅴ — Format & Tools (Your Choice)

Vestigia does **not** prescribe a tool.

You may use:

◆ Markdown files  
◆ a digital notebook  
◆ a private Git repository  
◆ a document tool  
◆ a physical notebook (if it suits you)  

What matters is that your records are:

⩪ dated  
⩫ sequential  
⩬ readable later  
⩭ honest  

Do **not** rewrite history.  
Do **not** polish entries for grading.

This guide values **process over presentation**.

---

## Ⅵ — What This Is *Preparing You For*

By following Guide ○ A, you are quietly preparing:

◆ your final report  
◆ your project presentation  
◆ your reflective writing  
◆ your portfolio or README  
◆ your ability to explain your work clearly  

You are reducing future effort by capturing truth *now*.

Vestigia is not something you “submit”.

It is something you **use**.

If you record honestly:
◈ your project becomes clearer  
◈ your writing becomes easier  
◈ your reflection becomes real  

Guide ○ A is where the work *actually begins*.

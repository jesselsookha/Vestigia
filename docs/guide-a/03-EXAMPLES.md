## Ⅰ — How to Read These Examples

Each example below contains three layers:

◆ **The Living Record** — what a student might actually write  
◇ **Individual ↔ Group Perspective** — how it supports both  
◈ **Annotated Commentary** — how this maps to:
  - Guide ○ A sections
  - subject deliverables
  - WIL and assessment expectations

These are not idealised reports.  
They are **snapshots of work in progress**.

## Ⅱ — Example ①  
### Engineering Project (Mechanical / Electrical / Systems)

**Project Context**  
Design and prototype a low‑cost water‑level monitoring system for a rural reservoir.

---

### Living Record — Week 3

**Date:** 12 March  
**Focus:** Sensor selection under environmental constraints

---

**Direction & Intention**

Initial intent was to use an ultrasonic sensor for non‑contact water‑level measurement to avoid submerged components and reduce maintenance risk.

---

**Decision & Trade‑off**

⩪ Selected ultrasonic sensor  
⩫ Considered pressure sensor and float‑based alternatives  
⩬ Non‑contact sensing aligned with safety requirements  
⩭ Risk accepted: sensitivity to surface disturbance

---

**Work Performed**

✻ Reviewed sensor datasheets  
✻ Performed bench‑top range tests  
✻ Calculated expected accuracy margins  

---

**Problem / Failure**

Readings became unstable when surface agitation was introduced during simulation testing.

---

**Reflection in Motion**

This revealed an untested assumption about surface stability.  
Future decisions must validate environmental context earlier.

---

### Individual ↔ Group Perspective

**Individual**

◆ Demonstrates engineering judgement  
◆ Provides evidence for personal reflection  
◆ Supports viva or oral questioning  

**Group**

◇ Records why this sensor choice was made  
◇ Prevents re‑litigating earlier decisions  
◇ Feeds directly into design justification sections  

---

### Annotated Commentary

**Guide ○ A Mapping**

⩪ Direction & Intention → Section Ⅲ.Ⅰ  
⩫ Decisions & Trade‑offs → Section Ⅲ.Ⅱ  
⩬ Work Performed → Section Ⅲ.Ⅲ  
⩭ Problems & Failure → Section Ⅲ.Ⅳ  
⩮ Reflection in Motion → Section Ⅲ.Ⅴ  

**Subject / Assessment Alignment**

◆ Design justification in proposal/report  
◆ Risk analysis and constraints discussion  
◆ WIL evidence of professional reasoning  
◆ Supervisor meeting discussion points

## Ⅲ — Example ②  
### Computer Science / IT Project (Software Development)

**Project Context**  
Build a full‑stack task‑management application (REST API + mobile frontend).

---

### Living Record — Sprint 2

**Date:** 27 March  
**Focus:** Authentication architecture

---

**Direction & Intention**

Authentication must support both mobile and web clients while minimising security risk.

---

**Decision & Trade‑off**

⩪ Adopted OAuth‑based authentication  
⩫ Considered custom JWT solution  
⩬ OAuth reduced implementation and security burden  
⩭ Loss of fine‑grained control accepted

---

**Work Performed**

✻ Integrated OAuth provider  
✻ Implemented token handling  
✻ Updated user schema  

---

**Problem / Failure**

Access tokens expired silently on mobile clients due to refresh misconfiguration.

---

**Reflection in Motion**

Assumed frontend and backend token lifecycles would align automatically.  
This highlighted a gap in my understanding of auth flows.

---

### Individual ↔ Group Perspective

**Individual**

◆ Shows architectural reasoning  
◆ Supports individual reflection marks  
◆ Provides material for technical interviews  

**Group**

◇ Explains why OAuth was chosen  
◇ Assists onboarding new team members  
◇ Anchors collective system architecture  

---

### Annotated Commentary

**Guide ○ A Mapping**

⩪ Architecture direction → Ⅲ.Ⅰ  
⩫ Technical decisions → Ⅲ.Ⅱ  
⩬ Implementation work → Ⅲ.Ⅲ  
⩭ Debugging failure → Ⅲ.Ⅳ  
⩮ Learning reflection → Ⅲ.Ⅴ  

**Subject / Assessment Alignment**

◆ System design and implementation sections  
◆ Testing and evaluation write‑ups  
◆ WIL evidence of real‑world development practice  
◆ Sprint reviews and supervisor progress checks

## Ⅳ — Example ③  
### Education Project (Non‑Technical Discipline)

**Project Context**  
Design a literacy intervention programme for Grade 4 learners and evaluate its impact.

---

### Living Record — Design Phase

**Date:** 18 April  
**Focus:** Teaching strategy selection

---

**Direction & Intention**

Initial approach relied on worksheet‑based activities, chosen for predictability and ease of delivery.

---

**Decision & Trade‑off**

⩪ Shifted to collaborative group‑reading sessions  
⩫ Considered individual reading tasks  
⩬ Group work supported engagement objectives  
⩭ Harder classroom management accepted

---

**Work Performed**

✻ Observed literacy lessons  
✻ Reviewed intervention literature  
✻ Developed structured session plan  

---

**Problem / Challenge**

Dominant learners limited peer participation during pilot session.

---

**Reflection in Motion**

Revealed that collaboration without structure can reproduce inequality.  
Next sessions will include role rotation.

---

◆ Supports reflective practice requirements  
◆ Provides ethical and professional insight  

**Group (if collaborative research)**

◇ Aligns intervention rationale  
◇ Documents classroom adaptation decisions  
◇ Strengthens evaluation consistency

## Ⅴ — Example ④  
### IT / UI‑UX & Marketing Strategy Project

**Project Context**  
Develop a user‑centred marketing strategy and UI prototype for a mobile fitness application.

---

### Living Record — Research & Ideation Phase

**Date:** 6 May  
**Focus:** User personas and conversion goals

---

**Direction & Intention**

Primary goal is to increase onboarding conversion from free trial to paid subscription.

Initial assumption was that usability alone was the main barrier.

---

**Decision & Trade‑off**

⩪ Focused on motivational UX patterns (gamification and progress feedback)  
⩫ Considered feature‑expansion strategy  
⩬ UX‑driven approach addressed emotional engagement  
⩭ Risk accepted: impact harder to quantify early

---

**Work Performed**

✻ Conducted user interviews  
✻ Developed three personas  
✻ Sketched onboarding wireframes  
✻ Mapped customer journey stages  

---

**Problem / Failure**

Early prototype testers understood features but lacked motivation to continue past onboarding.

---

**Reflection in Motion**

This shifted my understanding from *usability* to *perceived value*.  
Marketing messaging must work with UX, not after it.

---

### Individual ↔ Group Perspective

**Individual**

◆ Captures design rationale  
◆ Supports reflective and portfolio assessments  
◆ Provides evidence of user‑centred thinking  

**Group (design + marketing team)**

◇ Aligns UX and campaign strategy  
◇ Records why certain personas were prioritised  
◇ Supports coherent branding and messaging  

---

⩭ User feedback problems → Ⅲ.Ⅳ  
⩮ Insight and reframing → Ⅲ.Ⅴ

# Vestigia — Guide ○ B  
◆ Foundations: From Records to Meaning

## Ⅰ — The Transition Between Guide ○ A and Guide ○ B

Guide ○ A helped you **capture the work as it happened**.

You recorded:
- decisions
- changes
- problems
- progress
- learning in motion

Guide ○ B begins at a different point.

It assumes you now have:
◈ traces of thinking  
◈ records of activity  
◈ fragments of insight  

The purpose of Guide ○ B is **not** to add more content.

Its purpose is to help you decide:

◆ what matters  
◇ what explains the project best  
◈ what demonstrates growth and contribution  
◉ what should be shown — and what should remain private  

This is the transition from **recording** to **interpreting**.

---

## Ⅱ — Why Interpretation Matters

A common misconception in final‑year projects is:

> “If I document everything, the final narrative will emerge automatically.”

It does not.

Raw records are:
- detailed
- chronological
- locally meaningful

Showcases, reports, portfolios, and presentations must be:
- selective
- coherent
- understandable to others

Guide ○ B exists because **not all recorded work deserves equal visibility**.

Interpretation is the act of:
◆ choosing significance over completeness  
◇ narrative over chronology  
◈ clarity over volume  

This is not removal — it is **responsible curation**.

---

## Ⅲ — Records Are Not the Same as Evidence

Guide ○ A produces **records**.

Guide ○ B works toward **evidence**.

These are not the same.

---

### Ⅲ.Ⅰ — Records

Records capture:
- what happened
- when it happened
- how you experienced it

They are often:
- rough
- personal
- incomplete
- exploratory

They are valuable — but not all are suitable for presentation.

---

### Ⅲ.Ⅱ — Evidence

Evidence explains:
- why decisions were made
- how problems were solved
- what was learned
- how contribution can be demonstrated

Evidence is:
◆ chosen  
◇ contextualised  
◈ explained  

Guide ○ B teaches you how to move from one to the other.

---

## Ⅳ — Two Sources of Material (And Why Both Matter)

Your final showcase, summary, or portfolio artefact should emerge from **two distinct sources**:

---

### Ⅳ.Ⅰ — Vestigia Records (From Guide ○ A)

These provide:
◆ insight into thinking  
◇ decision rationales  
◈ learning trajectories  
◉ personal contribution  

They answer:
> *How did I think through this project?*

---

### Ⅳ.Ⅱ — Project Artefacts (From the Subject)

These include:
- project reports
- designs
- code
- prototypes
- evaluations
- presentations
- meeting notes
- WIL documentation

They answer:
> *What did the project produce?*

---

Guide ○ B exists to help you **connect** these two sources — not confuse them or duplicate them.

---

## Ⅴ — Individual Ownership in Group Projects

Most final‑year projects are partly or fully collaborative.

However, **Guide ○ B is always concerned with the individual showcase**.

This is intentional.

You are expected to:
◆ present your understanding  
◇ explain your decisions  
◈ demonstrate your learning  

While still:
- acknowledging collaborators
- respecting shared outcomes
- referencing the collective project

Guide ○ B will help you:
◈ extract *your* contribution honestly  
◈ link to group artefacts appropriately  
◈ avoid overstating or underselling your role  

This balance is a professional skill — not a contradiction.

---

## Ⅵ — What Guide ○ B Does *Not* Do

Guide ○ B deliberately avoids:

- templates to fill in  
- examples to copy  
- fixed formats  
- prescribed section headings  

This is to protect:
◆ authenticity  
◇ disciplinary variation  
◈ intellectual ownership  

Your final artefact should sound like **you**, not like Vestigia.

---

## Ⅶ — What Guide ○ B Will Help You Do

By the end of Guide ○ B, you should be able to:

◆ extract the most meaningful insights from your records  
◇ decide what supports your narrative best  
◈ explain your contribution with confidence  
◉ prepare content suitable for:
  - showcases
  - portfolios
  - CV links
  - interviews
  - public sharing (e.g. LinkedIn)

All without rewriting your project from scratch.

---

## Ⅷ — A Note on Integrity and Restraint

Good interpretation includes **leaving some things out**.

Not every difficulty needs to be public.  
Not every note needs to be shared.  

Guide ○ B encourages:
◆ honesty, not exposure  
◇ clarity, not confession  
◈ professionalism, not performance  

What matters is that what **is shown** is accurate, reflective, and defensible.

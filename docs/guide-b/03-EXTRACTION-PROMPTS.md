# Vestigia — Guide ○ B  
◆ Extraction Prompts & Integrity Checks

## Ⅰ — How to Use This Document

This document is not something you complete line‑by‑line.

It is something you **consult deliberately** while preparing:
- an individual showcase
- a portfolio entry
- a public summary
- a professional README

Use the prompts below to **interrogate your material**, not to generate text mechanically.

If a prompt does not apply, skip it.  
If a prompt makes you pause, pay attention to that pause.

---

## Ⅱ — The Selection Mindset

Before extracting anything, hold this principle:

> **Your goal is not to show everything you did.  
> Your goal is to explain what mattered.**

Extraction is an act of judgement.

---

## Ⅲ — Decision Extraction Prompts

Use these prompts to identify decisions worth presenting.

Ask:

◆ Which decisions shaped the project’s direction?  
◇ Which decisions ruled out alternatives?  
◈ Which decisions required justification at the time?  
◉ Which decisions would raise questions if left unexplained?

Refinement prompts:

⩪ What was at stake when this decision was made?  
⩫ What information was incomplete or uncertain at the time?  
⩬ What trade‑off was accepted?  
⩭ What changed as a result of the decision?

Only decisions that **create consequence** belong in a showcase.

---

## Ⅳ — Learning Extraction Prompts

Learning is often implicit. These prompts help make it visible.

From your Vestigia records, ask:

◆ Where did my understanding change?  
◇ Which assumptions did I revise or abandon?  
◈ What did I struggle with that later became clearer?  
◉ Which skills matured noticeably during the project?

From your project artefacts, ask:

⩰ Where is that learning visible in the output?  
⩱ What improved because I understood more?  
⩲ What would have been weaker earlier in the project?

Learning is not a list of skills.  
It is **change over time**.

---

## Ⅴ — Problem & Resolution Prompts

Do not hide difficulty.  
Do not dramatise it either.

Identify problems that *produced insight*.

Ask:

◆ Which problems forced a change in approach?  
◇ Which failures led to better decisions later?  
◈ Which obstacles reshaped my plan, scope, or design?

Then connect:

⩳ What exactly went wrong?  
⩴ Why did the original approach fail?  
⩵ What was modified as a result?  
⩶ How did this affect the final outcome?

If a problem has no resolution or learning attached, it does not belong in your public narrative.

---

## Ⅵ — Contribution Prompts (Essential for Group Projects)

Your showcase is individual, even when the project was collaborative.

Use these prompts to extract contribution **without misrepresentation**.

From your records:

◆ What did I initiate rather than inherit?  
◇ What did I take responsibility for end‑to‑end?  
◈ Where did my decisions influence the group’s direction?

From project artefacts:

⩷ Which components would be incomplete or different without my input?  
⩸ Where is my contribution visible but undocumented?

Integrity checks:

- Could I explain this contribution clearly in an interview?
- Could a teammate recognise this description as fair?
- Am I claiming ownership or explaining involvement?

The goal is **defensible contribution**, not exclusivity.

---

## Ⅶ — Audience Alignment Prompts

Your narrative should make sense **beyond the subject context**.

Ask:

◆ What would a reader *not in my class* need to understand this?  
◇ What background knowledge am I implicitly assuming?  
◈ Where do I need to slow down and explain reasoning rather than results?

Then refine:

⩪ Which details clarify meaning?  
⩫ Which details create confusion without adding value?

Accessibility is professionalism.

---

## Ⅷ — Integrity & Restraint Checks

Before finalising what you present, pause and check:

◆ Does this accurately represent what happened?  
◇ Is anything misleading through omission or emphasis?  
◈ Am I presenting growth, not perfection?  
◉ Have collaborators been acknowledged appropriately?

Ask yourself:

> *Would I be comfortable defending every claim here, verbally and factually?*

If the answer is uncertain, revise.

---

## Ⅸ — What Not to Extract

You are encouraged to leave out:

- routine activity with no insight  
- work done exclusively by others  
- raw notes that require extensive explanation  
- repetition without development  

Exclusion is not dishonesty.  
It is discernment.

---

## Ⅹ — Preparing to Structure the Showcase

When these prompts have done their work, you should have:

◆ a small set of high‑significance decisions  
◇ a clear learning trajectory  
◈ a defensible account of contribution  
◉ evidence linked to authentic artefacts  

Only **after** this stage should you decide:
- format
- structure
- layout
- platform

Structure serves meaning — not the other way around.

---

## Ⅺ — In Summary

These prompts are not checklists.

They are **lenses**.

They help you see:
- what matters
- what explains
- what represents you honestly

Your final narrative should feel *inevitable*, not assembled.

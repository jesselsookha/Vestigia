# Vestigia — Guide ○ B  
◆ Integrity, Attribution, and Professional Representation

## Ⅰ — Why This Document Exists

Guide ○ B helps you transform records into narrative.

This document exists to help you do that transformation **responsibly**.

Final‑year projects sit at the intersection of:
- collaboration
- assessment
- professional identity
- public representation

Integrity is not assumed — it is *practised*.

---

## Ⅱ — Integrity Is Not About Caution, It Is About Accuracy

A common misunderstanding is that integrity requires:
- modesty
- under‑statement
- minimising contribution

This is incorrect.

Integrity requires:  
◆ accuracy  
◇ clarity  
◈ defensibility  

An accurate account may describe substantial responsibility.
A misleading account may describe very little — or too much.

Integrity is not self‑censorship.
It is **truthful representation**.

---

## Ⅲ — Individual Ownership in Collaborative Work

Even when a project is group‑based, **your showcase is individual**.

This is appropriate and expected when:
- applying for roles
- sharing work publicly
- discussing experience professionally

However, individual ownership must be clearly framed.

You are responsible for explaining:  
◆ what you were accountable for  
◇ how you influenced outcomes  
◈ where work was shared or collective  

You are **not** required to:
- present the entire project as your own
- suppress your contribution to avoid standing out

## Ⅳ — Attribution Principles

Use these principles to guide attribution decisions.

---

### Ⅳ.Ⅰ — Attribute People, Not Processes

Appropriate attribution focuses on:  
◆ collaborators  
◇ roles  
◈ responsibilities  

Avoid attributing:
- decisions to vague “we” statements
- outcomes to anonymous collective effort
- work without clarifying contribution boundaries

Clarity strengthens credibility.

---

### Ⅳ.Ⅱ — Attribute Artefacts Without Reproducing Them

You may reference:  
- shared repositories  
- project websites  
- group reports  
- collective presentations  

Without:  
- copying whole sections  
- reproducing group submissions verbatim  
- implying sole ownership  

Linking outward is often stronger than duplication.

---

### Ⅳ.Ⅲ — Use Language That Explains, Not Claims

Prefer language that describes **involvement and responsibility**, for example:  

◆ “I led the design of…”  
◇ “I was responsible for implementing…”  
◈ “I contributed by analysing and refining…”  

Avoid language that implies:  
- exclusive ownership  
- unqualified authorship  
- final authority over all aspects  

Precision builds trust.

---

## Ⅴ — Public Representation (CVs, LinkedIn, Portfolios)

When presenting your work publicly, consider:  

◆ Who is the audience?  
◇ What context do they lack?  
◈ What claims would require explanation if questioned?  

Use context framing:
- briefly explain the project scale
- note collaboration explicitly
- anchor claims in evidence

Visibility should never outpace truth.

---

## Ⅵ — WIL, Supervision, and Academic Alignment

Many final‑year projects operate under WIL or industry‑linked expectations.

Your Vestigia‑based showcase can support:  
◆ professional practice evidence  
◇ supervision discussions  
◈ reflective assessment alignment  

However:
- Vestigia does not replace official documentation  
- it complements existing artefacts  

Ensure that anything publicly presented:  
- does not breach confidentiality  
- respects institutional and partner agreements  
- reflects supervision feedback where applicable  

---

## Ⅶ — What Integrity Looks Like in Practice

Integrity is present when:  

◆ a supervisor could follow your narrative logically  
◇ a teammate would recognise your role as fair  
◈ you could defend every claim in conversation  

Integrity is compromised when:  

- contribution is exaggerated  
- collaboration is obscured  
- context is removed to enhance appearance  

If unsure, revise toward clarity.

---

## Ⅷ — Common Integrity Questions to Ask Yourself

Before publishing or sharing, pause and ask:  

◆ Have I clearly acknowledged collaboration?  
◇ Can I support each claim with evidence?  
◈ Have I avoided inflating impact through ambiguity?  
◉ Would I be comfortable explaining this to all stakeholders involved?  

These questions are safeguards, not obstacles.

---

## Ⅸ — Restraint Is Professionalism

Not everything you did needs to be public.

Leaving work out can be:  
◆ ethical  
◇ strategic  
◈ mature  

Strong professionals curate their narratives.
They do not present raw logs or unfiltered activity.

Vestigia supports **discernment**, not disclosure.

---

## Ⅹ — In Summary

Integrity in Guide ○ B means:  

◆ accuracy over ambition  
◇ explanation over assertion  
◈ collaboration without erasure  

Your goal is not to impress.

Your goal is to **represent your work in a way that is honest, defensible, and professional**.

That is what makes a showcase meaningful.
